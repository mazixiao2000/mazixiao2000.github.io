## From a Broad Concept to a Clear Core

The original concept asked the player to use a fishing rod for platforming, combat, and fishing, but pursuing all three directions at once diluted the experience. After a scope review, the team focused the project into a precision platformer and tested whether movement alone could sustain the game.

Early playtests still showed that the prototype lacked a strong sense of tension and decision-making, and the project was close to cancellation. I proposed a time-stop mechanic that lets players switch between high-speed movement and deliberate planning. The change improved control and created a reusable variable for future level design.

## Teaching & Difficulty Progression

I designed levels 1, 2, 3, 5, 12, 16, and several additional stages. Early levels isolate movement, grappling, and time control so players can understand each action in a low-risk environment. Later levels combine landing precision, chained inputs, compressed spaces, and higher failure costs.

![Visible landing zones, hazard boundaries, and movement trajectories provide immediate feedback](/assets/images/fling-gameplay.webp)

:::steps
- Isolate One Variable | Each teaching segment introduces one new action or judgment before adding combined pressure.
- Provide Safe Practice | Low-cost failure spaces allow repeated attempts and help players connect input with result.
- Recombine Skills | Previously learned actions are brought together into complete movement sequences that require planning.
- Vary the Rhythm | Short recovery moments are inserted between demanding sequences so fatigue does not obscure learning feedback.
:::

## Playtesting & Collaboration

During public playtests, we tracked failure locations, input misunderstandings, and repeated attempt counts. These observations led to changes in platform spacing, hazard boundaries, teaching order, and visual cues. Working in a small team required fast tradeoffs between design ambition and implementation cost while keeping gameplay, level structure, and feedback aligned.

## Takeaways

FLING taught me that the value of a core mechanic is not simply novelty; it is whether the mechanic can generate a broad set of clear level-design problems. Time stop worked because it affected movement rhythm, decision time, route planning, and psychological pressure at once, giving the project a foundation for sustained difficulty progression.
