## Project Introduction

**Game Introduction:**

*Starfield* is an open-world science-fiction RPG developed by Bethesda Game Studios. Built with the official Creation Kit, this project adds an original side quest that can be played independently within the game.

**Level Introduction:**

*Decaran: Become Human* is a *Starfield* side-quest level. The player is hired to investigate abnormal events at the automated Decaran III factory and gradually discovers the truth behind the robots’ awakening. As the story progresses, the player must ultimately choose between restoring factory production and helping the robots gain freedom. The choice leads to two distinctly different endings.

## My Work

### 1. Narrative Design

- Designed the complete side-quest story, mission objectives, and two-ending structure.
- Wrote NPC dialogue, quest text, and environmental narrative content.
- Used a moral dilemma to encourage players to consider the relationship between robots and humans.

### 2. Level Design

- Designed the factory’s overall spatial layout, player route, and exploration flow.
- Built a level rhythm that alternates combat, exploration, and puzzles.
- Used thematic zoning to strengthen spatial navigation and environmental readability.

### 3. Gameplay Design

- Configured enemies and combat encounters.
- Designed core gameplay elements including EMP weapons, laser mechanisms, and terminal puzzles.
- Used Papyrus Script to implement quest logic and event triggers.

### 4. Playtesting and Iteration

- Used multiple rounds of playtesting to continuously improve flow, combat difficulty, navigation, and environmental storytelling.
- Fixed scripting problems and completed the final version.

## Project Summary

### What Went Well

- The overall visual presentation was strong.
- I attempted and implemented several new design ideas.
- The level’s vertical spatial structure was especially successful.
- I continuously improved level flow in response to milestone reviews and playtest feedback.

### Even Better If

- Communication with stakeholders, including instructors and producers, could have been stronger.
- The laser-platforming gameplay was not fully successful and would benefit from further refinement.
- The narrative content was still insufficient.
- Combat difficulty was too low and lacked challenge.
- Some parts of the flow still required improvement.
- The laser mechanic was not used to its full potential.
- The exterior area was relatively underdeveloped.
- The level’s overall quality still fell short of my expectations.

### What I Learned

- Proactively communicate with stakeholders and adjust promptly in response to feedback.
- Plan development time more effectively.
- Gain a holistic understanding of the engine and tools as early as possible.
- Improve my ability to adapt to complex and unfamiliar development tools.
- Commercial game engines and internal tools are not perfect, so a designer must learn to work with their limitations.
- Complete an entire level project through a professional game-development process.
- Time and energy management are critical to project success.
- Design and improve level flow.
- Balance gameplay needs with believable architectural logic.
- Build game narratives that are more complete, clearly structured, and supportive of gameplay.
